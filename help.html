People

People are the core and backbone of Friendly Manager. The key thing to remember with Friendly Manager is that every single person in the system  has a user profile , no matter how much data you have recorded about each person. 

Friendly Manager has 7 levels of user access and this can be set from the user's profile. 

7 levels of user access  

  • No Login  
    This user can’t login - usually assigned to a person under 16. For this profile to be updated,a parent/guardian needs to be assigned as a 'Standard User' , or you as the admin need to do it.
  • Standard User
    Can login and manage their own account.
    Note  - If the member needs their account to be managed by a parent/guardian then you must set the parents/guardians account to ‘Standard User ’ - Any accounts linked to the parents/guardians account that has 'No Login'  will be managed by that parent/guardian.
  • Roll Taker  
    Can access the attendance for the classes they are apart of. 
  • Manager
    Has access to people in classes they are staff for.
  • Coordinator  
     Can see and edit all information for classes  - used mainly by committee members.
  • Financial Admin
    Can set up and edit fees for all members.
  • Club Admin
    Has access to everything - used by the main administrator/s of the club.
The ‘System Access ’ drop down is used to determine the level of access a user has for a team.

Note -  If a user has higher level access than 'No Login ' that person will require an email address. The Friendly Manager system requires all users with logins to have a unique email address, this cannot be changed.  

Registering Members

Every person connected to your club should have a profile in Friendly Manager. 
To register a new member: 
  1. Click ‘People’ .
  2. Click ‘Register Members’ .

     - you will see the registration form. 
  3. Enter the new member's details and select a term and a class.
  4. Click ‘Proceed to Parents/Guardians’ .
  5. Enter a parent/guardian or click the red ‘Remove’ button.
  6. Click‘Proceed to Summary’ or ‘Add another contact’ . 
  7. On the 'Summary' page you will be shown a summary of the member and parents/guardians you have registered.
  8. Review the members details and add any ‘Comments’ if applicable. 
    Note - If you have an Ezidebit account enabled  and there are  fees that need to be paid at this point you can create a transaction.
    If not skip to step 11.
    1. Select the payment method you would like. the fields will change based on the selection you make from the drop-down.
    2. If you have credit card enabled you can enter the credit card details of the person who is paying for the member's fee.
    3. Click ‘Signup!’ .

    Adding a Person Who is Not a Member

    Add a new person who is not a member - such as a 'Roll Taker', 'Manager', 'Coordinator' etc.

    1. Click the plus symbol found next to the search on the blue control bar. 

    2. Click 'New Person' .  

    3. Enter the name of the person - this is the only required field.
    4. Choose the system access for this person - Click here to learn more about system access levels.
    5. If the person has higher system access than 'No Login' they will require an email address .
    6. Fill in any other information you would like to.
    7. Click 'Save' .  

    Connecting a Parent/Guardian to a Member

    The parent/guardian option is used to link friends, family or other users to a members profile (i.e. linking a parent/guardian to a child’s profile). 

    There are three types of parents/guardians
    • Primary   - Normally the main point of contact and will have login access to the members's profile if the member is not managing their account them self.
    • Standard Parent/Guardian   - Enables the ability to have extra parents/guardians attached to the member.
    • Emergency Only   - A secondary person who should only be contacted in emergencies.

    Connecting a parent/guardian can be done three ways

    From a members profile

    1. Click 'People ' in the side menu.
    2. Click on the person you would like to connect to.
    3. Click 'Edit '.  
      .
    4. Down the bottom right corner you will see two buttons. If the person already exists in the system click 'Attach Existing ' otherwise click 'New Parent/Guardian '.
    5. A new parent/guardian box will appear. Enter the parents/guardians details and ensure the details are correct.
    6. Enter the  relationship to that person.
    7. Choose what type of contact they are.
    8. You can assign a 'System Access ' level.
    9. If you want the person you are connecting to receive email on behalf of the member then tick the 'Emails ' checkbox.
    10. You can repeat this as many times as needed. 
    11. Click 'Save '. 
    When registering a member

    1. Click 'People ' in the side menu.
    2. Click 'Register Players '.
      .
    3. Once you have added the members information you can click 'Proceed to Parents/Guardians '.
      .
    4. At this point, you can add the information for the new parent/guardian. You can add multiple parents/guardians if you want.
    5. Click ‘Proceed to Summary ’ or ‘Add another contact ’. 
    When adding a new person

    1. Click the plus button in the top control bar and select 'New Person '.
    2. Down the bottom right corner you will see two buttons. If the person already exists in the system click 'Attach Existing ' otherwise click 'New Parent/Guardian '. 
    3. A new parent/guardian box will appear. Enter the parents/guardians details and ensure the details are correct.
    4. Enter the  relationship to that person.
    5. Choose what type of contact they are.
    6. You can assign a 'System Access ' level.
    7. If you want the person you are connecting to receive email on behalf of the member then tick the 'Emails ' checkbox.
    8. You can repeat this as many times as needed. 
    9. Click 'Save '.

    Reset a User's Password

    A password reset can be done one of two ways . 
    The first way is completed by the user. We suggest you get your users to do it to save you time.
    How a user can reset their password. 

    1. Whenever a user  tries to login to the system there will be a link that says 'Forgot Password '.
    2. After clicking 'Forgot Password ' a popup will appear allowing the user to enter their 'login email address '.
    3. Once the user has entered the email address it will ask them to confirm they are not a robot.
    4. After confirmation they can then click 'Submit ' and this will send the user an email allowing them to reset their password.

    How to reset the password as an admin. 

    1. Click 'People ' in the side menu.
        
    2. Click the user's profile.
    3. In the middle of the screen click the orange button that says 'Reset Password '. 
    4. Confirm that you want to reset the password by clicking 'OK ' on the popup. 
    5. This has now reset the password and will send an email to the email address in the user's profile.
    Note - When you click 'OK ' the password will be changed and the old password will no longer exist. 


    Duplicates

    Friendly Manager has the ability to find duplicate names in the system and will notify you in two ways . 
    1. Email notification  
       When a member signs up to Friendly Manager you will automatically receive an email that shows you a confirmation of sign up.
      Note - These emails cannot be turned off.

      If Friendly Manager thinks there is a duplicate then you will receive a notification on the email alerting you of the duplication.

    2. Table of Duplicates
       As well as the email notification there is a page of duplicates that are in the system currently.

      Hover on 'People ' and click 'Duplicates Names '.

      This page shows you a complete list of all the duplicates in the system, if there are no duplicates the page will display 'No duplicates found! '.
      The table shows you the person's contact info, the role and the last time they logged in to the system. The information allows you to determine what to do with the duplicate. You can also click on the name of the person to be taken to their profile.

    Dealing with 'Duplicate Names'

    You can either choose to edit the duplicate profile details, merge those profiles or leave them as they are.


    To merge duplicated profiles
    Be careful when merging member profiles as when you merge a profile into another the profile you choose will be lost.

    1. Hover on 'People ' and click 'Duplicates Names '.
    2. Click 'Merge to # ' to the far right of the duplicated profile. Each 'Merge ' button will say, 'Merge to... ', and display the number allocated to the duplicated profile.
      Note - The 'Merge ' button you choose to press will transfer all information associated to that persons profile  (excluding profile/login details) to the other profile . The person you merge  will   no longer exist   after the merge.
    3. A popup will appear explaining the process to make sure you are happy with your decision.
    4. Click 'Merge '.

    Changing Class Age Limits

    The Class Age Limit is an optional annual cut off date for classes that have a set age range. For example, if you've set the age limit date for something like the 15th of February and have a class that only allows 10 to 12 year old's (or whatever age range you set), children turning 10 after that date won't be able to register for the class. Setting the class age limit helps you make sure you have the correct ages registering for your classes.

    1. Click thesettings cog icon in the top right corner of the screen next to the logout button.
    2. Click the 'Main Settings ' tab.
    3. Click 'Class Age Limits ' and select a new date.

    Terms

    Terms are the first thing you should create in your club. We recommend setting up all your terms for each year before the year starts. 
    Terms are a period of time you define for your club to run. For example if you are a sports club you may have a season that runs for certain months or seasons of the year. Every is assigned to a class for the duration of a term. 
    When the term ends, the member will no longer belong to the class unless they re-register or you re-assign them. 

    Define Term Sets

    If you have multiple codes you may need them to run on different term timelines which may possibly overlap. Without defining 'Term Sets ' you wont be able to overlap terms as this will create a conflict in the system. Currently you can define 4 different sets of terms.
    Creating Term Sets

    1. Click the settings cog at the top right corner of the screen next to the logout button.
    2. Click the 'Codes ' tab.
    3. Click 'Add ' or 'Edit ' depending on if you have 'Codes ' defined yet.
    4. From the 'Term Set ' drop down, choose either A, B, C or D.
    5. Click 'Save '.

    Transfer Process

    The transfer process is designed to allow you to easily pass players fromone term to the next , thus avoiding the need for each member to have to re-register. Transferring members to the next term will not remove them from their current term.

    To initiate a transfer process you need to have the new  term  created.  
    On the clubs dashboard(home screen) the 'Begin Transfer ' button will appear  40 days before the start of the next term.


    Also it is wise to set up the term fees for that term so when you transfer someone they will be invoiced that amount. 
    You as a club can override the fee at this point as well. 

    How do I transfer my members  

    1. On the dashboard, click 'Begin Transfer '.

    2. You will see a list of all the members in the class you have selected from the previous term. 
      .
    3. Select the class you want to put the members in to.
    4. If you have staff association enabled you will see the staff and will be able to set the staff for that term.

    5. The fee entered will be charged to the members.

    6. You can click on the 'Transfer ' button to manually transfer someone.
       
    7. If you don't want to transfer a particular person click 'Skip '
       - This will remove them from the table. 
    8. Once you have all the members you want to transfer, click 'Transfer All '. 
       
      You have now transferred all remaining people and created an invoice for each individual.
    9. Move to the next class using the drop down.
        
    10. Alternatively click the 'Next ' button to repeat the process.
       

    Note - If you don't want to transfer your members, simply click the 'Dismiss ' button. . 

    If you have dismissed the transfer process on accident and need to get back to it please open a ticket.

    Term Fees

    Every new term you will need to recreate the  term  fees for that  term.
     
    There are five different price options : 
    • Fixed Price  
       Fixed term price for every member of the class it is applied to.
    • Pro Rata Monthly or Weekly  
      A relative price applied to members joining part way through the term. Each week/month elapsed since the start of the term, the amount will reduce by a proportion according to the total number of weeks in the term (rounded up). Note - you must select 'By Week' or 'By Month' depending on your preferences.
    • Per Session  
      A fee automatically calculated based on the remaining attendance sessions in that term. E.g: if the fee is $10 per session and there are 10 attendances remaining the customer will be charged $100.
    • Monthly  
      A fee applied monthly. Members will be charged monthly the amount entered into the ‘Amount’ field. 
    • Nil Fee
      No fee.

    Add a Term Fee

    1. Hover on 'Fees '.
    2. Click 'Term Fees '.
    3. The top panel is where you create new term fees.

    How Friendly Manager Syncs with Xero

    Fees and transactions automatically send to Xero as soon as you create them in Friendly Manager.
    • Normal fees will create invoices.
    • Credit/refund fees will create credit notes.
    • Fee payment transactions will create payments for the associated invoice.
    • Refund transactions will create cash refunds for the associated credit note.
    • Automatic credit deduction transactions will do one of two things:
      • If the credit deduction came from a credit fee, an allocation will be created between the associated invoice and credit note.
      • If the credit deduction came from overpayment credit, a payment will be created for the associated invoice out of your designated Overpayment Account.
    • Contacts will automatically be created as necessary when creating Fees. If a contact of the same name already exists, you will be informed of the conflict and be given the option to either match to the existing contact or create a new one.
      Deleting fees and transactions in Friendly Manager will delete the associated item in Xero.
      • Due to current limitations of Xero, Friendly Manager is unable to delete allocations - this can only be done directly in Xero.
        Deleting people in Friendly Manager will NOT delete anything in Xero.
        •  
        Every hour, Friendly Manager will contact Xero and import or delete any of the following transactions that you may have created or deleted in Xero:
        • Invoice payments
        • Cash refunds
        • Direct payments into your Overpayment Account
        • Credit note allocations
        • Overpayment allocations
        • Prepayment allocations
          Invoices, credit notes or any other items you create or modify in Xero will NOT be imported into Friendly Manager.

          On a standard users account the user can:
          • Update any contact details. 
          • See all invoices and transactions.
          • View any awards assigned to them, if the correct settings is turned on. 
          • See any membership information, such as classes, they are enrolled in.
          • See any upcoming events.
          • Pay for any outstanding invoices, if 'Payment' is enabled. 
          • Register new people.
          • Re-Register people, when the sign up is turned on.
          • Click through to sponsor websites.

          Sending and Receiving Emails

          Emails to and from Friendly Manager  
          Friendly Manager uses the club admin's email address to communicate with the club. This can be changed by any club admin at any time. 

          1. Click the settings cog at the top right corner of the screen next to the logout button.
          2. On the 'Main Settings ' tab find the 'Primary Admin' drop down.
          3. From the drop-down choose the admin you would like emails to be sent to. 
          4. Click 'Save ' at the bottom right hand of the screen. 

          Your clubs designated public email address
          The default public email address, used for communicating with members, is the admin's email address. The public email can be changed at any time.

          1. Click the settings cog at the top right corner of the screen next to the logout button.
          2. On the 'Main Settings ' tab find the 'Club Email ' option.
          3. Enter an appropriate email address.
          4. Click 'Save ' at the bottom right hand of the screen.
